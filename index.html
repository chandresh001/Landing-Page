Sections

Foreign Earned Income Exclusion
Foreign earned income is exempt from tax (up to certain limits) and received for services while performing work in a foreign country during which your tax home is in a foreign country and either the bona fide residence test or the physical presence test is met.

Foreign Tax Credit
A foreign tax credit is generally offered by income tax systems that tax residents on worldwide income, to mitigate the potential for double taxation.

Taxation of Individual Non-Residents
Nonresident aliens are generally subject to U.S. income tax only on their U.S. source income, and subject to various special tax rules.

Taxation of Foreign Corporations
Foreign Corporations (or their respective subsidiaries) are generally subject to U.S. income tax only on their U.S. source income, or Effectively Connected Income with a trade or business, or branch, and subject to various special tax rules.

Tax Treaty Exclusions
A tax treaty is a bilateral (two-party) agreement made by two countries to resolve issues involving double taxation of passive and active income of each of their respective citizens.

Transfer Pricing
Transfer Pricing are the rules and methods for pricing transactions within and between enterprises under common ownership or control.

Subpart F
The Subpart F provisions eliminate deferral of U.S. tax on some categories of foreign income by taxing certain U.S. persons currently on their pro rata share of such income earned by their Controlled Foreign Corporations.

Global Intangible Low-Taxed Income

Passive Foreign Investment Companies
A passive foreign investment company (PFIC) is a corporation, located abroad, which exhibits either one of two conditions, based on either income or assets.
